- my story -

My life story, so far, can be told as a series of challenges and experiences, each one more difficult than the last. Some are self-prescribed; others not, but each challenge has prepared me for a new one. Four years ago I moved to Colorado with $200 in my bank account and nowhere to live. So, I put my craftsmanship to the test and built a backcountry hut in which to live. Then I scored a job as a ski instructor, not knowing how to ski. When spring came I used my earnings to buy a sailboat and lived aboard, not knowing how to sail of course. And after sailing season, I biked from the Continental Divide to the Great Lakes. When I returned from this expedition I was compelled to test my potential further. I had streamlined my learning style and become highly proficient at adapting to and overcoming new obstacles. I wanted an intellectual challenge; something rigorous, so I decided I would teach myself to code. For eight months I pushed forward in that pursuit until I landed the job and joined the booming tech industry in Boulder. And the road has gone onward and upward since then. Every day I test my limits and rise up with new experiences and knowledge under my belt. Do you have a challenge for me? Or maybe you wish to share an experience. Let's see where life can take us next.

Seth D. Muller
